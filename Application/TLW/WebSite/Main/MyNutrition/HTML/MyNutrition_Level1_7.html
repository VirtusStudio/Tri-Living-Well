Hunger Helpers

Hunger can be fickle, random, gentle or fierce. It’s something that differs from person to person, and isn’t consistent on a daily basis. The goal of this article is to help you understand the types of hunger you’re feeling, so you can be aware of when you’re eating and why.

Learning to read your hunger

Stomach Hunger: This is the most basic form of hunger and is very easy to identify. Quite simply, physical hunger indicates that your body needs food. You may feel a mild gurgling or gnawing in your stomach. When you’re really hungry, your stomach might even growl. Symptoms of physical hunger can also include a headache, irritability, or even a feeling of faintness. A general guideline to hunger is to go no longer than 4-5 waking hours without eating.


Mind Hunger: The mind is an active thing – sometimes too active. For all sorts of reasons, the brain can trigger feelings of hunger, even if your stomach is already full. Some people use food to relax or reward themselves, or to designate a break in their routine. If you find yourself constantly asking if it’s time to eat, it’s probably a mind hunger type of desire.


Emotional Hunger: Our emotions are tricky things that can lead us to act physically. When we get frustrated we might want to kick or punch something. Other emotions can cause us to feel hungry and eat when we really don’t need food. You might notice emotional hunger triggered when you feel tired, thirsty, bored, upset, stressed, or angry. Emotional hunger can also kick in when you need a break from work or you’re trying to put off doing work.

Hunger Homework

To understand your patterns even better, record your feelings of hunger before, during and after eating. When you’re through, see if you can find a pattern by asking yourself questions like these:

  • What time of day do you feel the hungriest?
  • How long do you usually wait between meals?
  • How often did you feel hungry in a 24-hour period?

It might be hard to read your body if you’re a long-time dieter, because you’ve worked hard to numb yourself to sensations of hunger and satiety. As you begin to get to know your body, you’ll be better able to anticipate and manage your hunger.
