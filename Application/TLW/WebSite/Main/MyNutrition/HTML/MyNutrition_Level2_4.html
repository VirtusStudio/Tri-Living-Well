Stage 4 – Feel Your Hunger – Living

 

 

Feel Your Hunger

 

If you’re always half on a diet, watching calories and counting fat grams, you may have lost touch with your body’s natural way of regulating food intake: hunger and satisfaction sensations. And that’s a shame. Being able to identify and satisfy true hunger can change your relationship to food. The goal should be not to follow any prescribed eating plan blindly but to learn to eat when hungry and stop when satisfied. This self-knowledge allows you to maintain a natural weight without obsessing over food or the scale.

 

 

Reading your body’s hunger signals

 

Learning when to eat

 

The first step in reading your body’s hunger signals is to develop an internal meter to help identify when you’re eating for the right reasons (physical need) or the wrong ones (emotional need). People who are used to rigid diets might be ignoring important hunger signs. That can lead to overeating in the end. Conversely, people who are used to eating for comfort may misinterpret their feelings as true physiological hunger.

 

Here are some tips to help you identify true physical hunger

 

 

                Yellow Lights—Think first  

 

  • In the middle of an activity—writing a report at work, or doing your laundry on the weekend—you start thinking about food.  Should you suppress that thought? Not necessarily. This could be the first sign that your body needs nourishment.
  • Ask yourself: How long has it been since you had something to eat? Would any other activity (like a walk or chat on the phone) satisfy your desire?

 
 
 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 


 

Learning when to stop

 

Once you become sensitive to your body’s satiety signals, you’ll know when to stop eating. Your body does give you clues about when to put the fork down.  But tuning in to these clues can be tricky, because we are conditioned to listen to our heads, not our bodies.

 

Here are some tips for deciding if you’re satiated or if you really need to keep working your fork.

 

 

 

 

Red Lights—All done!    

 

  • True satiety is felt in the body. It often comes in phases. First, you might feel pressure or some fullness in your stomach. About 20 minutes after you start eating, the physical signs of hunger may diminish or you may be less interested in your food.
  • Your waistband is getting tight. If you rely on discomfort to help you stop, you’re out of touch with your body’s signals.
  • You’re really stuffed. Dieting may have given you a feast-or-famine mind-set. You may fear that you won’t get enough food or that a moderate amount won’t satisfy you long enough, so you overeat. 

 
 
 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 


 

 

 

 

 

 

 

Getting back in touch

 

Pick one meal a week when you can dine without distractions.  Don’t count calories. Don’t worry whether the foods you’re eating are “good” or “bad.”  Instead, pay attention to what your stomach feels like before, during, and after eating.  Try to discern the phases of fullness and satisfaction. You can use the hunger scale below. Before and after eating, rate your hunger on a scale from zero to ten. Soon, you’ll begin to develop a heightened awareness of your true hunger.

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

Hunger and Satiety Scale

 

 

0

 

 

Starving

This is the point where you feel sick: faint, dizzy, headache, moodiness, irritability, and inability to concentrate.

1

 

 

Extremely hungry

A slight headache is forming and you are beginning to feel sick.

2

 

 

Very hungry

Anything edible looks good to you.

3

 

 

Typical hunger

This is what you should feel when you wake up in the morning. You should want to eat in about 15 - 20 minutes.

4

 

 

Slightly hungry

You can probably put off eating, but you know you may need a meal within half- an- hour.

5

 

 

Neutral

Not too hungry and not too full.

6

 

 

Slightly full

You no longer feel hunger. You are still digesting and feel like you can eat a little more.

7

 

 

Satisfaction

Your hunger is definitely gone and you are completely full.

8

 

 

Very full

You’re not uncomfortable, but you have definitely eaten enough to feel stuffed.

9

 

 

Uncomfortably full

Your stomach actually becomes distended.

10

 

 

Stuffed

You’re uncomfortable or even in pain. You might even have to unbutton the top button of your pants.

Sample Recipe

 

Roasted Turkey, Sun-dried Tomato and Basil Wraps

 

 

Serves: 4

 

Ingredients:

 

1/2 cup cream cheese, low-fat whipped
2 tablespoons chopped sun-dried tomatoes or 6 sundried tomatoes, rehydrated and chopped
10 basil leaves, chopped
4 large whole wheat flour tortilla
3/4 pound sliced smoked turkey breast
4 lettuce leaves, green leaf, bibb or romaine, shredded

 

 

 

Directions:

 

1. In a small mixing bowl, combine the cream cheese, sundried tomatoes and basil.

 

2. Lay the tortillas out and spread 1 tablespoon of the cream cheese mixture on each of them. Divide the turkey among the tortillas and spread the remaining cream cheese mixture on top of the turkey.

 

3. Divide the shredded lettuce among the tortillas and tightly roll each tortilla into a cylinder, ending with the seam side down. (The wraps can be stored in the refrigerator for up to 3 days.)

 

4. Cut the wraps in half on the diagonal and serve.
