Stage 6 – Fat is Fabulous – Living

 

 

Fat is Fabulous!

 

Fat – the kind you eat, not the kind that squishes over those jeans that fit just a month ago – is probably the most maligned of all nutrients. You may be more confused by its appropriate place in your diet than all the other nutrients combined. You know you need fat in your diet but you’re afraid its use will inevitably lead to weight gain. Perhaps you are unsure how to choose the right types or amounts of fat for optimal nutritional value. Remember that fat is essential for energy, hormone function, and structuring and insulating your body. And, of course, it can make food taste great!

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 


Function

Benefit

 

As energy

Fat provides the body’s storehouse of energy. It yields more energy and is more efficient than stored carbohydrates (glycogen). Most muscles receive their energy from stored fat, sparing stored protein for tissue building.

 

As body mass

Fat insulates us to preserve heat and maintain body temperature. Without it, we would have no way to keep our organs or nerves in position or protected from injury and shock.

 

In body chemistry

Feeling good owes a lot to fat. It helps us feel full by depressing gastric secretions and decreasing gastric emptying. It also increases endorphins, known as the happy hormone. In fact, fat creates hormones as well as nerve cells. It also aids in transporting and absorbing fat-soluble vitamins (A, D, E, and K).

 

 

 

How to Measure Fat

 

All good things in moderation: Some fat is an essential part of a healthy diet, but too much can contribute to heart disease, cancer, and obesity. Obesity carries the risk of its own associated diseases, including diabetes and high blood pressure. The American Heart Association suggests limiting fat to no more than 30% of your total daily calories.

 

But be sure to get enough: Diets that are extremely low in fat can also be dangerous, because fatty acids are essential to life. Here are some symptoms of fatty acid deficiency:

 

  • Dermatitis (skin inflammation)

 

  • Neurological changes:

 

    • Numbness
    • Weakness
    • Inability to walk
    • Blurred vision

 

  • Specific to children

 

    • Learning deficits
    • Poor growth in infants

 

Fat Finding Mission

 

 

Having the proper amount of fat in your diet will provide you with all its benefits while preventing the dangers of fat overload. But that doesn’t mean scrutinizing every food label and choosing only fat-free items. Here are some tips for finding the right balance of fat from healthy sources:

 

·    Stick with unsaturated fats—skip the butter and cook with vegetable oils like safflower, soy and olive oil more often.

 

·    Keep your meat choices lean

 

·    Choose low-fat dairy products— enjoy fat free or low fat milk as well as the wide selection of healthy low-fat cheeses that are now as readily available and equally delicious as the full fat variety

 

·    When eating chicken, be sure to remove the skin

Sample Recipe

 

Raspberry-Almond Oatmeal

 

 

Serves: 1

 

Ingredients:

 

1 cup water
1/2 cup oatmeal
1 tablespoon raspberry jam
1/2 tablespoon slivered almonds

 

Directions:

 

1. Combine the water and oatmeal in a small saucepan.

 

2. Cook, stirring occasionally, over medium heat for 7 minutes. Stir in the jam and almonds.

 

3. Spoon into a serving dish. Serve warm.
