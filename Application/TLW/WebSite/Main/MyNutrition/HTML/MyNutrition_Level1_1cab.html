Tips for Eating Out

Going out to eat should be a nice experience, but deciding what to order can be stressful, especially when you’re managing your weight. You want to be conscious of the fat and calories hiding on the menu, even in selections that seem healthy. But you don’t have to eat rabbit food either. It’s important to feel satisfied and not deprived. It’s even okay to have a treat once in a while!

 

Here are some tips to avoid common restaurant mistakes

 

Watch your portion size: Don’t order the “supersize” meals or be tempted by “value meals.” They can double or even triple the fat and calories of the regular size. Instead, try splitting a main dish or dessert with a friend. Some restaurants even offer lunch-sized (or smaller) portions during dinner. Try to avoid “all you can eat” specials, because you may end up eating more than you planned. If you do eat more than you planned, don’t get upset with yourself – it happens. Just try to cut back on fat and/or sodium the next day.


How it’s cooked can make all the difference: Try to avoid deep-fried foods, which are very high in fat. This includes breaded meats such as chicken and fish filet sandwiches. Instead, look for options that are steamed, baked, roasted, broiled, grilled, or poached. These can have the same amount of flavor, but will use a lot less oil.


Beware of sneaky calories: Sometimes the “extras” have more calories than the main dishes. Toppings like bacon or cheese and dressings can be loaded with calories, so order them on the side and use sparingly. Rather than pouring on salad dressing, try dipping your fork into the dressing and then into the salad.

Drinks can be another source of unconsidered calories. Stay away from milkshakes, malts, and regular soda. Even juices and alcohol (including beer and wine) can cause the calories to add up. Diet soda and water are both better choices.

Salad bars might seem healthy, but they can also contain sneaky calories. To choose wisely among the offerings, pile on fresh vegetables and fruits — and go easy on the cheese, eggs, croutons, bacon bits, and meat. And remember: Always put your dressing on the side.

 

Feel like royalty: It’s nice to be catered to when dining out, especially when you’re trying to stick to healthy options. Don’t be afraid to ask for special requests. Ask for substitutions when available. For example, order a baked potato instead of French fries. Perhaps the chef will steam a vegetable instead of sautéing it in butter. You’ll never know if you don’t ask!

Nutrition Program Tri Stage 1
1600 to 1800 Calorie Sample Meals

# 1

 

Breakfast

 

1 English muffin 

1 c. egg whites, scrambled

1 slice low-fat cheese (American, Swiss, or provolone)

8 oz. orange juice

 

Morning Snack

 

1 c. mixed fruit

1/4 c. trail mix

 

Lunch

 

Peanut Butter and Jelly Sandwich

2 Tbsp. creamy peanut butter

1 Tbsp. 100% fruit spread

2 slices whole wheat bread

1 medium apple

 

Afternoon Snack

 

1 chewy oatmeal-raisin granola bar

 

Dinner

 

8 large cooked shrimp

1/2 c. brown rice

1/2 c. cauliflower (boiled, no salt)

1 Tbsp. Smart-Balance “light buttery spread”

 

Evening Snack

 

1/2 c. low-fat frozen yogurt

 

TOTAL: 1585 calories
