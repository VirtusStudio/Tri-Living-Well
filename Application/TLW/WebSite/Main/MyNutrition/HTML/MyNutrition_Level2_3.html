Omega-3 Fatty Acids

 

They don’t sound like something that would be good for you. Or maybe they sound like a new fraternity on campus. But actually, Omega-3 fatty acids are absolutely amazing for brain function and development and can help prevent many common diseases. They’re found in fish, nuts, soy, and even grains.

 

 

Making Omega-3’s Work for You

 

What are they? Omega-3 fatty acids are polyunsaturated fats that are essential to the human body. Because your body doesn’t produce them, you have to get them through the foods you eat. If you don’t get enough of these fatty acids from your diet, you could develop a deficiency. Symptoms might include dry skin, poor circulation, fatigue, depression and memory problems. Stay clear of these problems by enjoying delicious foods rich in this fatty acid.

 

 

 

 

Adults: Research has revealed many health benefits of Omega-3 fatty acids. They may help lower the risk factors for arthritis, heart disease and cancer. Fish oils in particular have been shown to reduce blood pressure, reduce triglyceride levels, and improve circulation — all of which can help lower the risk for heart attacks.

Infants and Children: There are even experiments using the fatty acids to help children with brain dysfunction from ADHD and autism to depression and anxiety. A recent scientific study at The Women's & Children's Health Research Institute has demonstrated that high doses of fatty acids administered to pre-term infants via their mother's breast milk or infant formula can help their mental development.

How much do you need?

 

 

The American Heart Association recommends most adults eat fish with high Omega-3’s at least twice a week. The exceptions are children and pregnant or nursing women. Due to the amount of mercury found in fish, these groups should limit their fish consumption.

 

Sources like flaxseed, soybeans and walnuts are safe bets for everyone. However, as we all know, too much of a good thing can be bad for you. Omega-3’s are no different—too high of a dosage can be toxic. So if you decide to take supplements as a way to get Omega-3’s into your diet, do so only under the supervision of a doctor.

Sample Recipe

 

Super Moist Pumpkin Bread

 

Serves: 8

Serving Size: 1 slice

 

Ingredients:

 

1/2 stick unsalted butter
1/2 cup sugar
1 large egg
1/2 cup canned pumpkin
1/4 cup nonfat, plain yogurt
1/4 cup honey
1 teaspoon vanilla extract
1 cup flour
1 teaspoon baking powder
1 teaspoon cinnamon
1/4 teaspoon salt
1/4 teaspoon ground ginger
1/4 teaspoon ground allspice

 

Directions:

 

1. Preheat oven to 350°F. Spray a bread pan with nonstick cooking spray.

 

2. In the bowl of an electric mixer, beat together the butter and sugar on high speed until smooth. With the mixer on low speed, add the egg and combine. Add the pumpkin, yogurt, honey and vanilla and combine until smooth.

 

3. In a separate bowl, combine the flour, baking powder, cinnamon, salt, ginger and allspice. Fold this mixture into the pumpkin mixture and combine until smooth.

 

4. Pour into the prepared pan and place in the center of the oven. Bake for 45 to 50 minutes or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
