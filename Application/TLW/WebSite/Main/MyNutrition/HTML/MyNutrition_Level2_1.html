Snacking for Success

 

Snacking often gets a bad rep because many people snack in unhealthy ways. But snacking can be a great way to pack in all the nutrients you need for the day—especially if you’re unable to squeeze those nutrients into your meals. In other words, it’s okay to snack as long as you snack healthily and in moderation. In fact, it can actually put the kibosh on yo-yo dieting, because it prevents your body from feeling starved. Here are some tips for healthy and even power snacking.

 

               Tips for Healthy Snacking

 

 

 

Treats: Everyone feels like tortilla chips or cookies once in a while, and that’s okay. Just remember to keep moderation and portion size in mind. Eating two cookies is not the same as munching the whole box of Thin Mints.

 

Wet Weight: It’s easy to forget that drinks have calories, too. From soda and coffee drinks to beer and wine, these empty calories can add up quickly. Again, keep moderation in mind.

 

Prepare for Pleasure: It’s 3 o’clock, two more hours to go, your head feels so heavy you’re afraid it’s going to land on the keyboard, and lunch is a distant memory to your grumbling gut. Then you remember the vending machine in the hall. Sound the alarm! This is the worst kind of snacking—the impulsive kind.  To avoid it, carry an extra piece of fruit to the office or keep some microwave popcorn at your desk. Having a healthy option prepared can be the antidote to unhealthy snack attacks.

 

Add it in: Instead of thinking of snacks as “extras,” plan them into your daily diet. You can even think of snacks as mini-meals. That way you won’t end up eating more calories than you intended.

 

Reasons Matter: Be sure you’re snacking because of hunger, rather than anxiety, boredom, or fatigue. Snacking for the wrong reasons can lead to overeating. Maybe you just need a break from your current task. Try taking a walk, getting a drink of water, stretching or taking a nap instead of snacking mindlessly.

Sample Recipe

 

Honey Roasted Chicken with Rosemary and Dijon

 

 

Serves 4

 

Ingredients:


1 whole chicken, about 5 pounds
salt to taste
freshly ground black pepper
1 tablespoon Dijon mustard
3 sprigs of fresh rosemary
1/4 cup honey
1 lemon, zested, halved and juiced
4 cloves garlic, peeled
1/2 yellow onion, quartered

Directions:


1. Preheat oven to 375°F. Rinse the chicken under cold water and pat dry with paper towels. Season inside and out with salt and pepper.

2. Place the chicken in a large roasting pan.

3. Roughly chop two sprigs of the rosemary. In a small bowl, mix together Dijon mustard, honey, chopped rosemary, lemon juice and lemon zest.

4. Place the remaining sprig of rosemary, a lemon half, the onion quarters and garlic in the cavity of the bird. Using a pastry brush, coat the outside of the bird with the lemon honey glaze.

5. Place the roasting pan in the oven and baste the chicken every 15 minutes with any remaining glaze. Roast until a thermometer inserted into the thigh reaches 180° and juices run clear, about one hour. Remove and discard the skin. Serve hot or at room temperature.

 

 

 

Exchanges:  1 serving = 6 protein, 1 starch, 1 fat
