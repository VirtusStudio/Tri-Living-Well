What is Iron?

Iron is an important mineral that your body needs to function well. It helps build red blood cells by building hemoglobin, which carries oxygen to your cells. Cells use that oxygen to create energy.

When your body doesn’t have enough iron, you may feel tired and weak. But there are lots of delicious foods that are rich in iron, and we’ll talk about them later in this article.

Here are some iron basics

How much iron do you need? People need varying amounts of iron based on gender as well as life stages and choices. Adult men and older folks need the least amount of iron. Babies and kids need extra iron as they grow. Menstruating women need to replace the iron they lose during their cycles. Vegetarians must also monitor their iron intake because the human body doesn’t absorb plant iron easily. See the chart above to figure out how much iron you’ll need every day to stay healthy.

 

Understanding iron absorption: We only absorb about 10% of the iron contained in the foods we eat, and it’s easier for our bodies to absorb it from some foods than others. Our bodies absorb iron more efficiently from animal products like meat, than from plant foods such as vegetables, fruits and grains. You can increase your body’s ability to absorb iron from plant foods by eating them in combination with small amounts of lean meat, poultry or fish. Combining iron-rich foods with foods rich in Vitamin C can also facilitate absorption. On the other hand, coffee and black tea prevent iron from being absorbed by the body, so be sure not to overload on these.

 

Supplementing your iron intake: Besides eating foods naturally rich in iron, you can increase your iron intake in several different ways. Today many foods are made with iron-enriched flour, including baked goods, pasta, and cereals. Even cooking in cast iron skillets adds a small amount of iron to your food. You can also take iron supplements, but only do so if advised by a health care professional, because too much iron can be harmful. Iron supplements should be taken with food rich in Vitamin C for maximum absorption. Make sure you wait two hours before or after taking calcium supplements or multivitamins.

 

The math on Iron and Vitamin C:

It can be hard to figure out just how much iron you’re getting, especially if you’re eating foods without labels. And remember, you can increase your body’s ability to absorb the iron if you combine it with some vitamin C. Here’s a list of some good choices for both.
