Strength Training – Stage 2

Over the next two weeks, you will continue practicing the exercises covered in Stage 1. This is part of building a strength-training foundation.

Why keep your exercises the same? Since you’ll likely be making changes to your exercise, diet, and overall health and wellness routines, familiarity with this workout will ease an adjustment. Additionally, learning these exercises will physically prepare you for the free weights and exercise variations in the upcoming weeks.

By the end of this stage, you should notice some increased strength and maybe some new “tone” in your muscles.

Let’s get going!

Here’s what you’ll do in your workout

The basic drill: This routine includes 11 exercises that will take 45 minutes or less and should be completed twice a week. Perform two sets of each exercise listed. Aim for 15 repetitions in the first set and a minimum of 10 repetitions in the second set — but aim for 15. Keep your movements slow, controlled, and move through the fullest range of motion possible.

 

 

Here’s how to do it:

 

1. Find the machine or station and complete 15 repetitions. (Set One)

2. Rest for one minute.

3. Repeat the exercise aiming for 15 repetitions (Set Two)

4. Take a 1-minute rest before moving to the next machine or station

 

A word about weights: In the initial set of workouts, it is important to experiment to find the appropriate weight for each exercise. Be conservative. Each exercise should feel tough — but not impossible. Remember that it’s more important to move the weight safely than the amount of weight being lifted.

 

A few tips when working with weights:

 

• If the weight load feels too heavy, stop immediately.  Remove some weight right away.

• If the weight load feels too light, finish your set first. Add more weight

prior to your next set.

 

• As a rule of thumb, increase the weight when you can do 15 repetitions for both sets in proper form.

Notes from the Coach

·         It’s natural for your muscles to feel tired and sore, especially when you are new to an exercise routine. If you have lingering pain for several days, you should call a doctor.

 

·         Stay with this workout, and you’ll be less prone to injury. Remember that your body needs time to adjust to increased physical demands. Strength training, like other exercise programs, begin with foundational work. Put in the time now, and soon you’ll find yourself full of stamina and strength!

 

·         During this stage, concentrate on your breathing. Breathe in when lowering weights and breathe out when lifting them. Remember, it’s normal to find yourself breathing hard during workouts.

 

·         Don’t be concerned with the scale. You may gain a few pounds in the first few weeks, but it’s most likely muscle weight. This new muscle will (eventually) burn body fat. Stay consistent.

 

·         If you’re doing your workouts quickly, that’s great! When you complete your workouts in less than 45 minutes, it means you’ve gotten your heart-rate up. If you want to kick up your workout, rest for 30 seconds in between sets (rather than a minute).
