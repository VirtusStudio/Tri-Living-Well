How to Boost Your Metabolism

Workouts may burn calories, but the majority (60%) of calories is actually shed through one’s metabolism. The goal of this article is to introduce the metabolism and show how eating habits can affect energy.

The metabolism is responsible for creating energy. Our bodies get that energy from food and the nutrients stored in our bodies.

We burn this energy in three different ways:

1.   Resting Metabolic Rate (RMR): This is the speed at which your metabolism burns calories. That accounts for 60% of the calories you burn every day.

2.   Physical activity: This is energy that’s being used due to physical activity. That accounts for only 30% of the calories you burn in a day.

3.   Digestion: This is simply the process of eating and digesting. 10% of your calories are burned when you digest and absorb your food.

Because it accounts for over half the calories you burn in a day, maximizing your metabolism will help you lose weight. Relying only on dieting is often ineffective. Restricting calories can actually cause your body to decrease its metabolic rate in effort to conserve energy.

 

Here are some tips to optimize your metabolism:

Eat by the clock: Trite as it may sound, breakfast is the most important meal of the day. When you sleep at night, your metabolism slows down because you haven’t eaten in 8 – 10 hours. Eating a healthy breakfast will “jump start” your metabolism and help you to burn energy more efficiently throughout the day.

Eat all day: Eating smaller, more frequent meals will help stabilize your blood sugar and provide a steady source of energy throughout the day. This will help you maintain high metabolic activity. Don’t wait too long between meals or skipping meals, because this will slow your metabolism. Try to eat every 4 to 5 hours.

Refuel without food: Be sure to get plenty of rest and drink plenty of water. Because your metabolism needs water to effectively burn energy, you need to stay hydrated. Never allow yourself to be thirsty, and aim for 7 to 8 hours of sleep per night. The amount time you sleep affects how your body processes and stores carbohydrates. It can also impact hormones that regulate your appetite and body weight.

Exercise is key: Physical activity not only burns calories on its own; it also revs up your metabolism. Aerobic exercise, preferably in the morning, will boost your metabolism for the day. Strength training 2 to 3 times per week helps build muscle. Muscle burns more calories than fat, so the more muscular you become, the more calories you will burn at rest. That means even when you’re sitting on the couch, you continue to reap the rewards of your workout.
