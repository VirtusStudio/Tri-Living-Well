Monitoring Your Hunger

 

Normal eating means eating only when you’re hungry — and stopping when you’re satisfied. Many people have trouble figuring out when it’s time to stop eating.

 

Hunger is tricky because it varies from person to person and day to day. It’s important to monitor both your hunger and fullness to avoid a negative cycle of overeating.

 

We beat ourselves up when we eat too much, but when we don’t eat enough, we might end up binging or eating whatever food is immediately available. This is extremely unhealthy. As you learn to recognize your body’s hunger and fullness cues, your eating habits will become smart and satisfying.

Monitor your eating

 

Now, it’s time for the fun part. Let’s do some monitoring and use the chart to help understand our bodies and our hunger tendencies. Over the next week, rate your hunger on a scale 0 – 10, using the chart above. Make sure to record your rating before you eat and after you eat, and jot down the situation or circumstances surrounding your eating decisions. When you’ve got a week’s worth of notes, take a look at the chart.

 

0-3 Level
If you find yourself on the scale between 0 and 3, you’ve got an increased chance of overeating and/or binging and purging.  Watch out for situations where you’re this hungry and if you see patterns, change them.

 

4-8 Level

This is a good level rating. You aren’t waiting too long to eat, and you’re not eating too much. Being in the middle of the scale means you’re likely to eat reasonable portions and make healthy food choices.

 

9-10 Level

If you find yourself on the scale between 9 and 10, you’ve probably eaten too much. Very often, this level corresponds with waiting too long to eat or maintaining unhealthy eating habits. Make a note of the times you’ve gotten to this level and search for patterns. Is it the time of day? Is it the people you’re with? Is it a certain meal? Examining these variables may help you make changes.
