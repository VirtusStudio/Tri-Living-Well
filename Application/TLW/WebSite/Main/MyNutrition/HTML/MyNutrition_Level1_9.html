Nutrition for Fitness

It’s not just elite athletes who need to think about eating for enhanced physical performance. Even people who are new to exercise should consider their food as fuel. Adequate calories, protein, and nutrients are essential for achieving and maintaining strength, flexibility, and endurance.

Eating for fitness goes beyond just eating. You must make sure you eat moderate portions of a varied and balanced diet. Proper nutrition throughout the year can help you to train better and feel better. In this article, we’ll examine eating for optimal fitness, rather than simply worrying about the scale.

Getting the right “Fitness Fuels”

Water is the basic building block of our bodies. 60--70 percent of our bodies are made up of water, but we lose it rapidly when we sweat during workouts. If you don’t replace those fluids as you exercise, you could notice impaired performance within a single hour. So keep a water bottle with you or make frequent stops at the fountain.

Carbs are another must if you’re going to work out, as they provide the best muscle fuel. Despite all the anti-carb diet hype, the truth is your body needs them for energy. When your body doesn’t get enough carbs, it will turn to protein for an alternative energy source. That means the protein you need to build muscle mass will be used up during your workout. But don’t go too crazy at the bagel shop unless you’re  a marathon runner or long-distance tri-athlete. Most moderate exercisers will benefit from a regularly balanced meal.

 

Things to watch out for

Even though your muscles need protein to build muscle mass, you don’t need to load up on it. Muscle consists of about 20% protein, and the rest is all water (another reason to stay hydrated). Even fitness enthusiasts should eat protein in moderation, keeping it below 30% of day’s calories. Another thing to avoid? Taking too many vitamins. Never exceed the recommended dietary allowances for vitamins and minerals. It won’t improve your workout and some vitamins can become toxic when taken in excess.

Timing is everything

It’s essential to fuel the body both before and after exercise. After your hit the gym, try to have a meal or snack rich in both carbohydrates and proteins. Ideally you should grab a snack within 30 minutes of your workout to allow for faster recovery and to replenish lost nutrients.
