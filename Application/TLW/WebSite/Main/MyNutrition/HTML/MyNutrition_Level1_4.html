Losing weight with healthy portions.

An important facet of a sustainable lifestyle change is learning moderation. Any swing from one extreme to another can be overwhelming for your body, which might revolt. 

Healthy eating is often termed the “golden mean.” That’s because it’s the middle between the extremes of excess (over eating) and deficiency (not eating enough). Here are some tools for understanding and exercising portion control.

 

Learning Portion Control

 

Portion Control Benefits: Healthy eating means eating a variety of foods that provide the nutrients you need to maintain your health. When combined with being physically active and maintaining a healthy weight, eating well can help your outlook or mood. It can also help boost your energy level. On the other hand, overeating is linked to several diseases and conditions, including obesity and diabetes, and can lead to a shorter life span. A single episode of overeating can impact the body’s metabolism and cause blood sugar to fluctuate.

Portion Control Basics: To maintain a healthy weight, it helps to know what a normal portion looks like. Portion control is defined as understanding how much a serving size is and how many calories a serving contains. The failure to control portions is often caused by emotional factors such as boredom or depression.

Understanding Emotional Eating: Eating for reasons other than hunger, such as anxiety or frustration, is called emotional eating. About 75% of overeating stems from an attempt to suppress negative feelings. When emotionally troubled, people tend to seek comfort foods, which are often high in calories, sugar, salt and fat. Identifying and understanding emotional triggers can help you resist the urge to overeat. You can also use your daily diary to record your triggers. This will help you identify patterns that lead to overeating.

 

 

 

 

Portion Control Tips: Overeating can become habitual, whether your triggers are emotional or situational. Break the habit by finding some healthy alternatives, such as taking a walk, when you feel the urge to eat for the wrong reasons. When it’s time to eat for the right reasons, use some positive strategies for controlling your portions when it’s time to pick up the fork. Here are some great approaches for fighting the urge to eat unnecessarily and for eating sensibly sized meals.
