The Power of Protein

 

Most people get all the protein they need in their everyday diets (about 10% -25%) from the total calories they eat. But that doesn’t mean they are getting the best bang out of their protein choices. By watching the fat content and building meals that include complete proteins, you can get the essential amino acids your body needs without taking in too many unnecessary extra calories.

Why is eating proteins so important?

 

Because your body can not produce the essential amino acids and it needs to garner these nutrients from the food you supply. You can load up on these essential amino acids by eating complete proteins.

 

Smart Protein

 

Complete Proteins: To get the most punch for your protein, try to eat complete proteins—those that contain all of the essential amino acids. Most animal-based protein like meat and dairy are complete proteins, as is soy. But you can also create complete protein combinations from foods that don’t have all the essentials. Pair several incomplete proteins in one meal or enjoy several over the course of the day. Either way is equally effective.

 

Try some of these examples:

 

 

 

 

Soy: Soybeans are the only known plant source that is a complete protein. They can be used to create soymilk, tofu, and many other foods that can substitute for or compliment animal based proteins. That makes it a great choice for vegetarians and meat eaters alike. Adding soy can even help decrease cholesterol. Just be sure to choose products low in fat, sodium, and, of course, cholesterol.

 

Fat Content: Because some proteins can be saturated in calorie laden fat, it’s important to choose wisely from the many protein options available in grocery store aisles and restaurant menus.

 

Here are some tips for eating lean:

 

  • Choose poultry or fish instead of red meat

 

  • Remove skin from poultry

 

  • Cut away all visible fat from meat and poultry

 

  • Use egg whites instead of the whole egg

 

  • Choose low-fat or fat-free milks, yogurt, and cheeses

 

Be Wary of Supplements: Protein supplements and amino acid pills are often advertised as body building aids, but there is no scientific proof that they are effective. These supplements can even be dangerous, because consuming too much protein can tax the kidneys and liver, which process it. It’s both healthier and safer to meet your protein needs through food sources versus artificial supplements. And a whole lot tastier!

Sample Recipe

 

Sweet Potato Soufflé

 

Serves: 10

 

Ingredients:

 

4 large sweet potatoes
2 tablespoons olive oil
1/2 cup skim milk
1/2 cup dry sherry
1/2 cup brown sugar
1/2 cup frozen orange juice from concentrate
1 teaspoon nutmeg
1 teaspoon cloves
1 teaspoon cinnamon
1 pinch salt
4 large egg whites
2 tablespoons sugar
2 oranges cut in thin slices

 

Directions:

 

1. Preheat oven to 350°F.

 

2. In a large pot, cover the sweet potatoes with water and bring to a boil. Boil until tender.

 

3. Drain and peel the potatoes and place in a large mixing bowl. Mash well.

 

4. Add the olive oil, milk, sherry, brown sugar, orange juice, nutmeg, cloves, cinnamon and salt. Beat well with a mixer.

 

5. Spoon mixture into an oiled, ovenproof gratin dish.

 

6. Bake until the potatoes are hot, about 25 minutes.

 

7. While the soufflé is cooking, make the meringue. In a clean bowl, whip egg whites until almost stiff. Gradually add the sugar and continue to beat until soft peaks form.

 

8. During the last five minutes of cooking the soufflé, top with the meringue mixture.

 

9. Return to oven and remove when the meringue is lightly browned. Garnish with the sliced oranges.
