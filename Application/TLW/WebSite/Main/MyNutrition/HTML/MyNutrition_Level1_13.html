Fiber Facts for Fitness

Admittedly, fiber – or roughage -- has kind of a stodgy image, but high fiber foods can be delicious. Did you know that popcorn is a great choice for a high fiber snack? High fiber foods also tend to be excellent sources for complex carbs, vitamins, and minerals.

Diets that are high in fiber and low in fat can reduce the risk of heart disease and some cancers. Yet most Americans eat only half as much fiber as they should. The National Cancer Institute recommends 20-30 grams per day for good health.

 

What is fiber?

Dietary fiber refers to the indigestible parts of plants. Fiber can be water soluble or insoluble. Water insoluble fiber causes fermentation in the colon that can help prevent cancer. It also helps you produce waste faster. Try eating grains like bran cereals or whole wheat bread for a healthy dose. Water soluble fiber helps lower blood cholesterol, which can help you fight heart disease. Eat oats, legumes, and fresh fruits and vegetables to give your body a boost.

 

Fiber Sources

• Glorious grains: Grains are a great way to get more fiber because there’s so much variety. Be sure to choose whole grains from barley to couscous to bulgar to brown rice. When choosing bread, make sure it’s whole wheat and has at least two grams of fiber. Stay away from loaves with added caramel color. Try eating a high fiber cereal at least twice a week. Fig bars, bran muffins, oatmeal raisin cookies, and popcorn make great snacks. You can even sneak in a little extra fiber by adding wheat bran or wheat germ to recipes.

 

• Prime produce: Fruits and vegetables are tasty sources of fiber no matter how you eat them, but there are some tricks to getting the best benefits. Select whole fruits instead of juices and be sure to eat the skins on apples, pears, and peaches. Look for fruits and vegetables with edible seeds. You can even try dried fruits like figs, prunes, or raisins. Vegetables like corn, peas, and baked potatoes (eat the skin, too!) are great on their own. Or you can try adding them to salads along with garbanzo and kidney beans to kick things up a notch.
