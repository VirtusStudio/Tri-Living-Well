Strength Training – Stage 3

It’s month two and time to build on the strength-training foundation that’s been established!

Included are some new exercises to give your muscles a fresh challenge. The rest of the exercises will carry forward from the last stages. Over the next two weeks, work to increase machine weights on familiar exercises. Remain consistent, and notice that you are getting stronger.

Lastly, have fun with this! You’ve not only made the commitment, you’re actually doing it. Let’s shake things up.

Here’s what you’ll do in your workout


The basic drill: The program will utilize 12 exercises, should take about 45 minutes, and should be completed twice per week. You’ll perform two sets of 10-15 repetitions for each exercise. Remember to keep your movements slow, controlled, and move through the fullest range of motion possible.

 

A word about weights: Over the course of the next two weeks, consider adding some additional weight. Here’s a rule of thumb: When you’re able to complete 15 repetitions for both sets, add some weight for your next workout. As always, keep exercise movements slow, controlled, and move through the fullest range of motion possible.

Notes from the Coach

·         Experiencing a little soreness? This is to be expected as your body learns new exercises. Not to worry. This soreness will likely fade in time. Pay attention to the difference between soreness and pain, to help avoid sports injury.

 

 

·         As training intensifies, your muscles may feel tired after workouts. Your body is telling you to refuel and get some rest. If you listen to your body, you will be properly recovered when it’s time for the next workout.

 

·         Use proper form before adding extra weights. If you have questions about how to position yourself on a machine, click on the name of the exercise for a detailed description, or ask an instructor at your gym for technical assistance.

 

·         When doing repetitions, make sure to achieve 15 in the first set and strive for 15 in the second set. Remember you’re becoming stronger with each workout.

 

·         As your workouts become more challenging, you may find yourself breathing harder. This is normal. Muscles need oxygen, so in some instances, a strength training exercise can be “aerobic”, like running or climbing stairs.

 

·         In month one, we noted that you might experience an initial weight gain due to muscle increase. If losing weight is a goal and you’re still gaining — look closely at daily calorie totals to see if eating changes are necessary. Keep your program goals in mind and enjoy your budding strength during your daily routine.
