Eating Healthy Away from Home

 

Busy schedules mean most of us don’t have time for homemade meals every day. But that doesn’t mean what we eat can’t be healthy. Eating out has become a convenient and fast alternative to the traditional “sit-down” meal at home. In the past, that often meant loading up on calories.

 

Fortunately, many restaurants, including fast food chains, now offer healthier meal options.  The recent banning of hydrogenated oils in several cities has sparked many restaurants to use healthier oils and reevaluate the nutritional content of the foods that they serve. So you can always make healthy choices, wherever you eat.

 

 

 

Tips for Healthy Eating on the Go

        

 

Preparation is key: Pack healthy snacks to avoid the temptation of vending machines, pastries, and other unhealthy indulgences. Look for foods that are healthy and easily transportable. The following list offers some great on-the-run choices.

 

                                                                                                                       

 

 

Coffee can mean calories: All coffee beverages are not created equal. Many contain shocking amounts of calories, fat, and sugar. Choose regular coffee and add a small amount of sugar and low fat or skim milk. Avoid beverages made with lots of whole milk, sugary powders, or syrups. Skip the add-ons like whipped cream. And beware portion size; one serving of coffee is 8 ounces, but “small” in many chain restaurants means 12 ounces.

 

Work the menu: Some menu choices are healthier than others. Choose balanced meals that contain whole grains, lean protein, vegetables, and fruit. For example, a grilled chicken sandwich on a whole grain bun with lettuce and tomato contains more nutrients and is lower in fat than a cheeseburger on a white bun with mayonnaise.  Opt for grilled over fried foods and choose lean proteins such as fish, chicken, or beans. Ask for dressings and sauces on the side or leave them off altogether.

 

Portion size matters: Even a low fat, well-balanced meal can be a calorie trap if the portion size is too large. It’s a whole lot easier to keep eating when you’ve got a huge plate in front of you, and many restaurants serve extra-large portions. You can avoid that trap altogether by requesting half portions of the meal or bringing some home in a doggie bag. That way you won’t be tempted to keep eating what’s before you even after you’re satisfied.

 

 

Feel free to substitute: There are lots of easy substitutions that can help make your meal healthier. You can try them even if you’re indulging in a burger or other high-cal treat for your main meal. Don’t be afraid to ask your server to make the switch.

Sample Recipe

 

Strawberry-Peach Muesli

 

 

Serves: 4

 

Ingredients:

 

1 cup rolled oats
1/2 cup skim milk
1/2 cup sliced, fresh strawberries
1/2 cup fresh, sliced peaches cored, peeled and diced

 

Directions:

 

1. Place the oats and milk in a bowl. Cover and refrigerate overnight.

2. Top with strawberries and peaches when ready to serve.
